Road Safety Awareness

Every journey matters. Road safety is not just about following rules; it is about protecting lives. By being aware of our surroundings and following traffic guidelines, we can prevent accidents and ensure a safer environment for everyone.

Learn the Rules

Road Safety Rules

Adhering to these basic rules can save lives:

  • Wear a Helmet: Always wear a helmet while riding a two-wheeler to protect your head.
  • Use Seat Belts: Drivers and passengers must wear seat belts at all times.
  • Follow Traffic Signals: Stop at red lights and proceed only when the light is green.
  • No Mobile Phones: Avoid using mobile phones while driving to stay focused.
  • Respect Speed Limits: Drive within the prescribed speed limits to avoid accidents.

Safety Tips

For Pedestrians

Always use zebra crossings to cross the road. Look left and right before crossing, and never walk on the road while looking at your phone.

For Cyclists

Ride on the right side of the road. Use hand signals to indicate turns and ensure your bike has working lights and reflectors.

For Drivers

Keep a safe distance from the vehicle in front of you. Check your mirrors frequently and signal before changing lanes.
